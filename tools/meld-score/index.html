About MELD 3.0

MELD 3.0 was introduced in 2022 by Kim et al. to address sex-based disparities in liver transplant allocation. The original MELD score disadvantaged women because creatinine values are generally lower in females. MELD 3.0 adds sex-specific interaction terms and incorporates serum albumin.

Key Improvements Over MELD-Na

  • Adds sex-based coefficient (1.33 points added for female patients)
  • Incorporates serum albumin as a variable
  • Better calibrated for 90-day waitlist mortality prediction
  • Reduces sex-based disparity in transplant allocation
  • Adopted by OPTN/UNOS for U.S. organ allocation (effective 2023)

Score Interpretation

  • MELD < 10: Low severity. 3-month mortality ~2%
  • MELD 10-19: Moderate severity. 3-month mortality ~6%
  • MELD 20-29: High severity. 3-month mortality ~20%
  • MELD 30-39: Very high severity. 3-month mortality ~50-80%
  • MELD ≥ 40: Critical. 3-month mortality >70%

Constraints

  • Lab values are bounded: creatinine capped at 3.0 mg/dL (4.0 if on dialysis), minimum values of 1.0 for bilirubin, INR, and creatinine
  • Sodium bounded: 125-137 mEq/L
  • Albumin bounded: 1.5-3.5 g/dL
  • Final score rounded to nearest integer, minimum 6, maximum 40

Formula

MELD 3.0 = 1.33 (if female) + 4.56 × ln(bilirubin) + 0.82 × (137 - Na) - 0.24 × (137 - Na) × ln(bilirubin) + 9.09 × ln(INR) + 11.14 × ln(creatinine) + 1.85 × (3.5 - albumin) - 1.83 × (3.5 - albumin) × ln(creatinine) + 6 Bounds: Bilirubin, INR, Cr >= 1.0; Cr <= 3.0 (4.0 if dialysis) Na: 125-137; Albumin: 1.5-3.5; Score: 6-40
