About BMI

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple measure of body fat based on height and weight. While BMI does not measure body fat directly, it correlates moderately well with more direct measures of body fat and is a useful screening tool for weight categories that may lead to health problems.

WHO Weight Categories

  • Underweight: BMI < 18.5 kg/m²
  • Normal weight: BMI 18.5 - 24.9 kg/m²
  • Overweight: BMI 25.0 - 29.9 kg/m²
  • Obesity Class I: BMI 30.0 - 34.9 kg/m²
  • Obesity Class II: BMI 35.0 - 39.9 kg/m²
  • Obesity Class III: BMI ≥ 40.0 kg/m²

Ethnic-Specific Cutoffs

The WHO recommends lower BMI cutoffs for Asian populations, where metabolic risk begins at lower BMI values. For Asian adults, overweight is defined as BMI ≥ 23 kg/m² and obesity as BMI ≥ 27.5 kg/m².

Limitations

  • Does not distinguish between lean mass and fat mass
  • May overestimate body fat in athletes and muscular individuals
  • May underestimate body fat in elderly individuals with sarcopenia
  • Not validated for children or pregnant women (use age/sex-specific charts)

Formula

BMI = weight (kg) / height (m)²

For imperial units: BMI = 703 × weight (lbs) / height (in)²
