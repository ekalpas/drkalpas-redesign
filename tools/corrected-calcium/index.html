About Corrected Calcium

About 40% of total serum calcium is bound to albumin. When albumin is low, total calcium may appear falsely low even though ionized (free) calcium is normal. The corrected calcium formula estimates what the total calcium would be if albumin were normal (4.0 g/dL).

Normal Ranges

  • Total calcium: 8.5 - 10.5 mg/dL (2.12 - 2.62 mmol/L)
  • Ionized calcium: 4.6 - 5.3 mg/dL (1.15 - 1.33 mmol/L)
  • Normal albumin: 3.5 - 5.5 g/dL

When to Use

  • Any patient with serum albumin < 4.0 g/dL
  • Critically ill patients (frequent hypoalbuminemia)
  • Nephrotic syndrome, liver disease, malnutrition
  • Before diagnosing hypocalcemia or hypercalcemia

Limitations

  • Ionized calcium is more accurate when available
  • Correction is less reliable in extreme pH abnormalities
  • Not validated in patients with multiple myeloma or paraproteinemia
  • Correction factor of 0.8 is an approximation and varies across labs

Formula

Corrected Ca = Measured Ca + 0.8 × (4.0 - Albumin)

The Payne formula adds 0.8 mg/dL of calcium for each 1.0 g/dL decrease in albumin below 4.0 g/dL.

References
