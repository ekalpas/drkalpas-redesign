About the CKD-EPI 2021 Equation

The 2021 CKD-EPI (Chronic Kidney Disease Epidemiology Collaboration) equation was developed to provide a race-free estimate of GFR. It was endorsed by the NKF-ASN Task Force and replaces the 2009 CKD-EPI equation that included a race coefficient.

CKD Stages (KDIGO)

  • G1: eGFR ≥ 90 mL/min/1.73m² — Normal or high
  • G2: eGFR 60-89 — Mildly decreased
  • G3a: eGFR 45-59 — Mildly to moderately decreased
  • G3b: eGFR 30-44 — Moderately to severely decreased
  • G4: eGFR 15-29 — Severely decreased
  • G5: eGFR < 15 — Kidney failure

Key Differences from CKD-EPI 2009

  • Eliminates the race coefficient entirely
  • Uses new coefficients derived from diverse populations
  • May report lower eGFR for Black patients and higher eGFR for non-Black patients compared to 2009
  • Endorsed by NKF, ASN, and KDIGO

Limitations

  • Not validated in children (<18 years), pregnant women, or AKI
  • Less accurate at extremes of body size or muscle mass
  • Creatinine-based; cystatin C-based equations may be preferred in certain populations
  • Does not replace measured GFR when precision is critical (e.g., kidney donation)

Formula

eGFR = 142 × min(Scr/κ, 1)^α × max(Scr/κ, 1)^(-1.200) × 0.9938^Age × (1.012 if female) Where: Scr = serum creatinine (mg/dL) κ = 0.7 (female) or 0.9 (male) α = -0.241 (female) or -0.302 (male)
